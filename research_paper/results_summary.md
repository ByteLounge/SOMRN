## Discussion of Claims

**Claim 1: Proactive Dual Prediction**
CPQR simultaneously evaluates both congestion (via EWMA of queue depths) and link failure (via RSSI trend regression). As seen in the *Congestion Events* and *Proactive Reroutes* metrics, CPQR successfully anticipates and routes around both congested nodes and failing links before they result in packet drops. The *Proactive Reroutes* counter specifically quantifies how often the algorithm overrides the base Q-value to avoid an imminent link failure.

**Claim 2: Tabular Q-learning with Graph-Based Cold-Start Fallback**
The *Early PDR (first 60s)* metric demonstrates the effectiveness of CPQR's cold-start fallback mechanism. While pure Q-routing typically suffers from near-zero initial delivery rates until Q-tables are populated, CPQR utilizes a shortest-hop fallback strategy during its exploration phase. This allows it to achieve a competitive Early PDR that is comparable to or better than the baseline protocols, avoiding the "death spiral" of empty Q-tables.

**Claim 3: Multi-Objective Reward with Tunable Weights**
The sensitivity analysis demonstrates that the reward function weights ($\beta$ for congestion and $W_e$ for energy) can be independently tuned to optimize performance. The generated heatmap (*sensitivity_heatmap_pdr.png*) illustrates the optimal operational region for these weights under high-load and high-mobility conditions, proving that CPQR's multi-objective approach provides robust adaptability.
